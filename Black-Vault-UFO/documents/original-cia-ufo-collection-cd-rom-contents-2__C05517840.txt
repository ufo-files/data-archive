 [FBIS Report]       Russia: Krasnodar media highlights 17-22 Jul
 The following are highlights from Krasnodar's Kuban State TV
 and the Volnaya
 Kuban, Krasnodarskiye Izvestiya, Kubanskiye Novosti,  and
 Komsomolskaya Pravda
 Na Kubani newspapers during the period 17-22 July 2001:
 Political
 The Russian State Duma decision to adopt the Land Code has
 caused serious
 upheaval in Krasnodar Territory. Kubanskiye Novosti publishes a
 selection of
 readers' letters urging to recall from the Duma those Krasnodar

 The flats owned by State Duma deputies Igor Khankoyev and
 Vasiliy Teterin,
 who voted in favour of the Land Code, did not suffered from pogroms

 A reform of the Legislative Assembly is mulled in Krasnodar
 Territory.
 There is a proposal to replace multi-seat    electoral districts
 that elect 50
 deputies by single-seat districts to elect 70. Under the present
 system 12
 municipal entities do not have their deputy. Moreover,  the number
 of deputies
 is not sufficient to work in committees.  (Krasnodarskiye Izvestiya,
 18 July)
 Economic
 Krasnodar Territory governor Aleksandr Tkachev has appealed for
 law-enforcement and tax agencies to establish a preferential
 taxation mode for
 farmers for the harvest period.  It does not mean that they are
 exempt from
 taxes, electricity debts and payments to the regional fund, he
 said. They will
 meet their obligations after completing the harvest, he added.
 (Krasnodarskiye
 Izvestiya,  17 July)
 Payment of arrears to the regional food fund is a priority.  It
 will be
 especially taken into consideration in case of filing for
 bankruptcy. Currently
 farms owe some lm tons of grain to the fund.  (Kuban State TV, 17
 July)
 Over 5m tons of grain have already been harvested in Krasnodar
 Territory. An
 average yield is 4,600 kg from a hectare.  (Krasnodarskiye
 Izvestiya, 20 July)
